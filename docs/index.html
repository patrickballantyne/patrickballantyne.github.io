Dr. Patrick Ballantyne

Postdoctoral Researcher in Geographic Data Science

I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher based in the Geographic Data Science Lab at the University of Liverpool. Broadly speaking, I am interested in all things Urban Analytics and Geographic Data Science, with a specific focus on:

Retail and High Street Performance

Urban Mobility and New Forms of Data

Accessibility and Social and Spatial Inequalities

At the Geographic Data Science Lab, I am responsible for supporting the research activities of Prof. Alex Singleton and the Consumer Data Research Centre, which involves a mixture of research, teaching and industry engagement. Furthermore, I co-ordinate fortnightly ‘Brown Bag’ seminars, where we invite guest speakers from academia and industry to deliver talks about their links to Geographic Data Science. If you are interested in talking at one of these, please do get in touch!

I am also working part-time as a spatial data consultant for Liverpool City Region Combined Authority, developing an evidence base to support place-based interventions to address inequalities in the region. Specifically, I have been developing a series of urban indicators that highlight compound regional inequalities (transport, housing, economic development), which are being used by policymakers to design creative interventions in these places to tackle inequality.
